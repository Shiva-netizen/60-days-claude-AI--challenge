# Day 33 - Media Integrity Analyzer

## 🎯 Objective

Learn how Claude AI can generate interactive educational applications that improve media literacy by teaching users to analyze headlines, recognize emotional manipulation, evaluate information critically, and make more informed decisions in the digital world.

---

# 🚀 Project Overview

Every day we encounter hundreds of headlines, social media posts, videos, and articles competing for our attention.

Some are informative.

Some are emotionally persuasive.

Others exaggerate, oversimplify, or omit important context.

This project demonstrates how Claude can generate a complete interactive learning experience that helps users become more thoughtful media consumers through guided discovery rather than memorization.

---

# 📰 What is Media Literacy?

Media literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and interpret information before accepting it as accurate or sharing it with others.

Strong media literacy helps people:

* Think critically
* Reduce misinformation
* Recognize emotional manipulation
* Make informed decisions
* Evaluate sources objectively

# 🕵️ Challenge 1 – Headline Detective

Before starting, the simulator explains:

### What is this?

Headlines are designed to capture attention quickly.

---

### Why does it matter?

A headline often shapes our first impression before we read the article.

---

### Everyday Example

People frequently share articles after reading only the headline.

The simulator encourages users to pause and evaluate instead of reacting immediately.

---

## Interactive Activity

Claude generates:

* A fictional news headline
* A matching fictional article

The user answers:

### Would you click this?

* Yes
* Maybe
* No

Next, the user identifies:

* Exaggerated phrases
* Missing context
* Misleading wording

---

## Learning Reveal

After submission, the application displays:

### Headline Accuracy Score

Measures how accurately the headline represents the article.

---

### Highlighted Mismatches

Shows exaggerated or misleading wording.

---

### Explanation

Explains why certain wording may create a false impression.

---

### Fair Rewrite

Presents a more balanced and accurate headline.

---

### Key Takeaway

Summarizes the lesson in simple language.

# ❤️ Challenge 2 – Emotion Detector

The simulator introduces another important media literacy concept.

### What is this?

Words influence emotions before logic.

---

### Why does it matter?

Strong emotional reactions often reduce critical thinking.

---

### Everyday Example

Many viral posts become popular because they trigger anger, fear, excitement, or curiosity.

---

## Interactive Activity

Claude generates a fictional:

* Social media post
* Reel caption
* Article excerpt

The user answers:

### How did this make you feel?

Examples:

* Curious
* Angry
* Excited
* Concerned
* Inspired

The user also identifies which words influenced those feelings.

---

## Learning Reveal

The simulator explains:

### Intended Audience

Who the content appears to target.

---

### Emotional Goal

What emotional response the writer likely wanted.

---

### Persuasion Technique

* Emotional Language

---

### Highlighted Emotional Words

The application visually marks emotionally loaded phrases.

---

### Neutral Rewrite

The content is rewritten using objective language.

---

### Key Takeaway

A concise lesson explaining how wording influences perception.
